    • About Pele

    Pelé, born Edson Arantes do Nascimento on October 23, 1940, is widely regarded as one of the greatest football players of all time.

    With his exceptional skills, remarkable goal-scoring ability, and charismatic personality, Pele has left an indelible mark on the world of football.

    Throughout his illustrious career, Pele achieved unprecedented success, both at the club level with Santos FC and on the international stage with the Brazilian national team.

    His contributions to the sport have transcended the game itself, elevating football's popularity and inspiring generations of players and fans worldwide.

    History of Pele

    1958: Breakthrough in the World Cup

    Pele, at the age of 17, showcased his exceptional talent in the 1958 FIFA World Cup. He became the youngest player to win the tournament, scoring six goals and leaving spectators in awe.

    1970: Captaining Brazil to Glory

    As the captain of the Brazilian national team, Pele led them to a historic third World Cup victory in 1970. His dazzling skills and leadership on the field played a pivotal role in Brazil's success.

    1974: Retirement from Professional Football

    After achieving unprecedented success and leaving an indelible mark on the sport, Pele retired from professional football in 1974, marking the end of an era and the beginning of his legacy.
